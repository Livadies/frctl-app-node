# NKO operating-model notes

These notes are a discussion draft, not a charter or legal opinion.

## Mission wording

The organization can frame its purpose around universal access to auditable open digital infrastructure, digital literacy, secure remote work and public-interest research. Product freedom is better expressed through a recognized open-source license, a public governance process and a contributor agreement than through an absolute promise that could conflict with sanctions, export-control, personal-data or security law.

## Recommended separation

- **NKO** owns the mission, trademarks, open specifications, reference implementation and public grants.
- **Independent operator/cooperative** handles paid compute, billing, tax, abuse response and service-level commitments.
- **Accredited security partner** owns the formal evaluation/certification work package under a written scope.
- **Node contributors** opt in to bounded workloads with quotas, hardware isolation, transparent compensation and an immediate kill switch.

Do not describe contributed compute as mining. Use “voluntary compute pool” and begin with signed, metered jobs without tokens, proof-of-work or transferable crypto-assets. Adding payments, resale of third-party resources or digital assets requires dedicated tax, consumer, AML and telecommunications analysis.
